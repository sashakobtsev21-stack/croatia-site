## Official facts used

- Porezna uprava defines resident/non-resident for income tax: resident = permanent or habitual residence in Croatia; resident taxable income follows worldwide income principle; non-resident taxable income follows domestic/Croatian-source principle.
- Habitual residence can be a continuous or time-linked stay of at least 183 days in one or two calendar years; short interruptions do not automatically break the analysis.
- Porezna can use TU/TI questionnaires when determining change of tax residency status.
- 2026 annual income tax rates are set by local government units within statutory limits; Porezna published the 2026 rates page on 16.12.2025.
- National annual bands: lower band up to EUR 60,000 annually; higher band above EUR 60,000 annually. If a local unit does not set its own decision, default rates are 20% and 30%.
- Digital nomad stay is for third-country nationals working remotely for foreign employers/clients or a foreign-registered business; it is not for Croatian employers/clients.
- Income Tax Act Art. 9 digital-nomad carve-out: receipts from employment/activity for a foreign employer based on acquired digital nomad status are not subject to Croatian income tax.
- VAT registration threshold for companies rose to EUR 50,000 from 2025 according to Ministry of Finance materials; treated only as context, not a company guide.
