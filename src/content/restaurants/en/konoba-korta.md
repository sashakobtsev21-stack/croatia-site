A traditional konoba set in one of the quiet stone courtyards of Diocletian's Palace, just steps from Split Cathedral and the Peristyle. The menu leans into Dalmatian heritage cooking: pašticada (beef slow-braised in a sweet-and-sour sauce, served with gnocchi), peka (meat or octopus baked under an iron bell), crni rižot (black cuttlefish risotto) and grilled Adriatic fish and shellfish. Eating this kind of food inside the 1,700-year-old palace walls is the real draw, and reviewers consistently rate it well for both the setting and value, with vegetarian, vegan and gluten-free options on hand. Because it sits in the busiest part of the Old Town it fills up at peak hours — come early or reserve. Prices and hours shift with the season, so confirm on site.
