A new seasonal fast ferry line called **Kvarner Bridge** began operating on **24 June 2026**, linking Kraljevica, Crikvenica, Senj, Baška on Krk, Lopar and Rab, and Lun on Pag. For visitors, the useful part is simple: it adds a sea route across the northern Adriatic at the moment summer road traffic is getting heavier.

## What changed

Croatia's Ministry of Sea, Transport and Infrastructure marked the start of the line in Crikvenica, with the service operated by Jadrolinija's catamaran **Silba**. Croatia Week reports that the seasonal route runs until **30 August**, starting four mornings per week from Kraljevica with return departures from Rab; from **10 July**, it is due to move to a daily peak-season schedule.

The line connects several places that are awkward to combine by road in one day: the Crikvenica Riviera, Senj, Baška on Krk, Lopar and Rab, plus Lun on Pag. Tickets are sold onboard and through Jadrolinija's website and app.

## Why it matters for travellers

The tourist angle is practical, not just ceremonial. Kvarner summer roads can be slow, especially around Krk, Crikvenica and island ferry approaches. A passenger catamaran gives visitors another way to plan day trips or short hops without putting a car in the queue.

## Factcheck

The route, launch date, operator and itinerary are confirmed by the ministry statement dated **24 June 2026** and by Croatia Week's same-year coverage. Prices and timetables are operational details, so travellers should verify the live schedule with Jadrolinija before leaving for the port.
