A small, family-run konoba in Split's Varoš quarter — the old fishermen's neighbourhood just west of Diocletian's Palace and a short walk from the Riva waterfront. The cooking is unfussy coastal Dalmatian: grilled fish bought fresh at the market each morning, octopus, brudet (a slow Adriatic fish stew) and black cuttlefish risotto, dressed simply with olive oil, garlic and parsley. It earned a Michelin Bib Gourmand for exactly this — honest, well-sourced food at a fair price — and remains a place locals send friends to. The room is small and books out fast in summer, so reserve ahead. Whole fish is sold by weight; confirm the price and the day's catch with your server, and check current opening hours before you go.
